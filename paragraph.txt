Our 8-bit game The Cuban Misfire draws inspiration from military action games of the 80’s, 8-bit era such as Contra (1987) and Ikari Warriors (1986). These two military action games like ours, put players in the position of elite soldiers fighting through enemy territory against overwhelming odds. Like those games, The Cuban Misfire’s core mechanic centers on lone combatant fighting through hostile territory. We translated this into a Cold War themed game where you play as a Soviet operative who has infiltrated an American submarine, fighting against U.S. and British soldiers. The side-scrolling environment that we created directly references Contra's left-to-right combat style, while the weapon variety, including a firearm for precise ranged attacks and molotov cocktail for larger damage, is represented in Ikari Warriors, emphasis on using different tools to control the battlefield. The underwater submarine setting reinterprets the jungle and military base stages of those classic arcade games into something more claustrophobic and complex to reflect the paranoia and tension that defined the Cold War era.
